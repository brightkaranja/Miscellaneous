Quality analysis

It is the identifing of a problem, generating corrective action, verifing them and implimenting corrective action.

There are different types Quality Assurance:

  1. Preventive - It prevents defects and errors from occuring.
  2. Detective - Identifing and fixing errors occuring.
  3. Corrective - Fixing problems that arise during and after production.
  4. Assessment - Ensure the consistency and reliability of a product.

Some of the software development life cycles are

  • Waterfall model
  • Agile model
  • Prototype model
  • V-model
  • Big bang model

There are different types of testing. Examples are:

  1. Manual testing - It is the testing performed manually by a human tester.
  2. Automated testing - Testing that is executed using automated script.
  3. Functional testing - Validates and evaluate system attributes like performance, security and usability.
  4. Non-functional testing - Evaluates if the application works as expected.

UX research

UX Research involves the identifying what the users are going through and finding the most effective way of solving their problems.

There are types of research like the primary and the secondary.

Process involved are researching, desighning, testing, Launching and improving.

There are stages of Human Centered Design like emphathy, define, ideate, prototype, and test

There are the five key stages in the human centered design including:

  • Emphathy - This is the understanding of the users.
  • Define - This is the clear staging of the problems.
  • Ideate - This is the coming up with multiple solutions.
  • Prototype - This is the coming up with a simple version of the solutions.
  • Test - This is the getting of feedback from the real users.

Product management

A product manager identifies a customer need and the larger business objective that a product will fulfil.

The strategy phases in product management are:

  1. Strategy - This is the problem that is happening.
  2. Discovery - This involves the research and the developing of a product.
  3. Delivery - This is the deploying of the product to the users.
  4. Measure and improve - This is the testing and maintaining of the final product.

There are key skills that a product manager needs.

  • Understanding
  • Communication and collaboration
  • They should be problem solvers.
  • Should have strategic thinking.
